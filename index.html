About

I am a final-year PhD candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Maryland, College Park, advised by Dr. Zhicheng Liu in the Human-Data Interaction Group.

I have published at conferences and workshops at top-tier machine learning and HCI venues including EMNLP, IEEE VIS, EuroVis, CHI, NAACL and CVPR, and have internship experience at Amazon Web Services developing causal discovery framework for temporal data.

My research interests span event sequence analysis, machine learning, data visualization, causal inference, and vision-language models.

My dissertation, titled "Beyond Domain Boundaries: Enhancing Generalizability of Event Sequence Visual Analytics Research," focuses on developing domain-agnostic modeling, analysis and visualization techniques for sequential data.

I hold a B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology.

Outside of research, I enjoy reading (check out my Goodreads), traveling, hiking and exploring new cuisines.

I am on the job market and actively seeking research or engineering roles starting September 2025. Please reach out to me!

Research

Vision-Language Model Evaluation for Process Visualizations

EMNLP Findings 2025
VLM Benchmarking

Created the first comprehensive benchmark (ProcVQA) for evaluating Vision-Language Models on process visualizations. Featured 118 charts across 8 domains, revealing critical performance degradation patterns in state-of-the-art models when processing complex visual information.

Causal Inference in Temporal Event Sequences

Amazon Web Services Internship • ICANN 2025
Causal Inference, Transformer

Developed novel causal frameworks extending Rubin's causal model to temporal point processes. Created hybrid Transformer-CNN architectures achieving 88% reduction in RMSE and 90% in MAE on industrial maintenance data for detecting causal relationship shifts under distribution changes.

Paper

Multi-Level Multidimensional Event Sequence Analytics Frameworka

IEEE VIS 2024
Framework Development

Built comprehensive domain-agnostic frameworks through systematic analysis of 58 visualization systems across 16 academic venues. Created four-level hierarchical taxonomy establishing theoretical foundation for next-generation intelligent assistance tools and cross-domain knowledge transfer.

Paper Talk

Large-Scale Human-Centered Evaluation Studies

Human Studies

Conducted large-scale empirical studies with 180+ participants examining visual summarization techniques and collaborative writing behaviors. Developed insight-based evaluation protocols and established benchmarking methodologies for visual analytics systems across cultural groups.
